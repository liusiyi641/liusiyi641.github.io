About

I'm third year PhD student at Department of Computer and Information Science, University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Dan Roth

My research interest lies within the areas of natural language processing. Recently, I’m interested in studying various types of conflicts in the applications of Large Language Models (LLMs), including

  • hallucinations and alignments - LLM generations that are non-factual, toxic, or unsubstatiated/contradictory to the contexts [1]
  • knowledge conflicts - conflicts among contexts or between contextual and parametric knowledge of LLMs [2]
  • perspectives - conflicts between different sources of perspectives and opinions[5] [6]

Some of my other ongoing projects include:
  • Teaching models to learn from failures/feedback
  • Deepfake Video Detection
